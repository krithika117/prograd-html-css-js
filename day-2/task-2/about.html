Birth and Early Life

Chadwick Boseman was born on November 29, 1976, in Anderson, South Carolina, United States. He was raised in a middle-class family, with his mother, Carolyn, working as a nurse, and his father, Leroy, working in a textile factory. Chadwick had two older brothers, Kevin and Derrick.

During his childhood, Chadwick developed a passion for storytelling and performance. He showed a keen interest in acting and participated in school plays and community theater productions. His talent and dedication earned him a scholarship to study at Howard University in Washington, D.C., where he pursued a Bachelor of Fine Arts degree in directing.

While studying at Howard University, Chadwick was mentored by renowned actress and playwright Phylicia Rashad, who played a significant role in shaping his career. He also participated in the university's theater program and became involved in various stage productions, further honing his acting skills.

Chadwick's early life and experiences growing up in a predominantly Black community deeply influenced his worldview and understanding of racial identity. These experiences would later inform his roles and advocacy for representation and diversity in the entertainment industry.

Despite facing financial challenges during his studies, Chadwick persevered and graduated from Howard University in 2000. He then continued his education by attending the British American Drama Academy in Oxford, England.
